What is the blockchain ?

A blockchain is a decentralized digital ledger that records transactions across a network of computers in a way that makes the data transparent, secure, and extremely difficult to alter.

    Ideas, simply explained

    • Blocks: Transactions are grouped into blocks.
    • Chain: Each block is linked to the previous one using cryptographic hashes, forming a chronological chain.
    • Decentralized: Instead of being stored by a single authority (like a bank), the ledger is shared across many independent nodes.
    • Immutable: Once data is added to the blockchain, changing it would require altering all following blocks and controlling most of the network’s computing power.
    • Consensus: The network agrees on which transactions are valid using mechanisms like Proof of Work or Proof of Stake.

    Why blockchain matters

    • Eliminates the need for trusted intermediaries
    • Prevents double-spending in digital currencies
    • Increases transparency and trust
    • Enables cryptocurrencies (like Bitcoin) and smart contracts (like Ethereum)

    Simple analogy

    • Everyone can see the entries
    • No single person controls it
    • Once an entry is confirmed, it can’t be changed
